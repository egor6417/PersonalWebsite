Isaac Newton by Andrew May

This is a super short book, and by no means is a whole biography of Newton's life and work. I picked up this book because I wanted to find out about the creator of calculus and one of the greatest mathematicians of all time, but I got so much more from this book. There are a few important bullet points that stuck in my head for a week after I read this book-

  1. Newton lived in Cambridge for 37 years before he left it for the Royal Mint.
  2. Sir Isaac was against counterfeiting, and arrested one of the leading counterfeiters of the time, Wiliam Chaloner.
  3. He was a workaholic- when he was meeting his friends, he could walk into his study to get a bottle of wine, but get hit by an idea and start writing it down, forgetting his friends.
  4. He didn't agree with Aristotle, the Greek philosopher who based his assumptions on "Common sense", and Isaac wrote in his textbook that "Aristotle is my friend, but truth is a bigger friend"

9.875 OUTTA 10
