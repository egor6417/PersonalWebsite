Every Song Ever

Every Song Ever is a book that I found quite misleading. The prologue was about how listening to music evolved through the ages (i.e. concerts then vinyls then computers then MP3 players then iPods then Spotify) The main book, however, was about the properties of music a listener shall look out for, as shown in multiple examples. That was not very interesting to me, but maybe I wasn't the right person to read the book. This, however, is countered by the opinions on the back cover saying that this book will help anyone understand music better. Which, at least for me, it did not.

5 OUTTA 10
